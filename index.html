Anatomy of a Bond

Principal (Face Value)

$1,000

The initial loan amount the issuer promises to pay back when the bond's term ends.

Coupon (Interest Rate)

5%

The annual interest paid to you, the lender. A 5% coupon on a $1,000 bond is $50 per year.

Maturity Date

10yr

The "pay-back day" when the loan term ends and the principal is returned in full.

Understanding Bond Risks: The See-Saw Effect

Interest Rate Risk

The most common risk is the inverse relationship between bond prices and market interest rates. When new bonds are issued at higher rates, your existing, lower-rate bond becomes less valuable.

This chart demonstrates the "see-saw." As market interest rates rise, the price you could sell your bond for on the open market falls.
